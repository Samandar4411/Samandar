Mountains of Busan

Discover the 10 highest mountains in Busan, each over 200 meters tall. Learn about their unique features and enjoy beautiful images of each peak.

Documentation

This project highlights the top 10 mountains in Busan exceeding 200 meters in height. I selected this topic during Week 5 idea sourcing and developed a website showing each mountain with images and interactive buttons that reveal brief, useful facts. I researched heights, features, and locations of each mountain. Future goals include adding hiking trails, maps, and difficulty levels. This was completed independently as part of Week 11's solo project challenge.

1. Geumjeongsan (801.5 m)

Geumjeongsan is the tallest mountain in Busan, known for its historic fortress, temples, and scenic hiking trails with views over the city.

2. Baegyangsan (641 m)

Baegyangsan offers panoramic city views and is a favorite for locals. It's ideal for sunrise hikes and features observation platforms.

3. Hwangnyeongsan (427 m)

Hwangnyeongsan provides nighttime views of Busan and has a popular observatory deck. It’s easily accessible and offers gentle trails.

4. Jangsan (634 m)

Located near Haeundae, Jangsan offers spectacular views of the coastline. The peak is ideal for watching the sunrise over the ocean.

5. Eomgwangsan (594 m)

Eomgwangsan is part of the mountain chain extending from Baegyangsan. It is less crowded and perfect for quiet hikes.

6. Gudeoksan (560 m)

Gudeoksan borders Seo-gu and is famous for its nature reserve and panoramic views of the western parts of Busan.

7. Seunghaksan (469 m)

Seunghaksan is popular among locals for quick getaways and peaceful hiking. It offers great views of Busan’s inner districts.

8. Daeunsan (375 m)

Daeunsan is close to residential areas and is perfect for beginners. It's known for well-maintained paths and spring flowers.

9. Cheolmasan (380 m)

Cheolmasan lies between urban and rural areas. It offers peaceful hiking, temples, and seasonal wildflowers.

10. Sinseondae (306 m)

Sinseondae is known for scenic rock formations and spiritual legends. It's near Taejongdae and offers coastal views.
